Overview

This redesign project was hosted by Design for America at UCSD. This student-led organization connects students with non-profit organizations committed to utilizing design thinking to address social challenges.

My group (5 people) was matched with Project Art of Learning, a student-run 501(c)(3) nonprofit organization that provides free one-on-one online tutoring for students in grades K through 12. They aim to support students requiring ongoing education and tutoring, collaborating with organizations that serve low-income, immigrant, and refugee students.

The process began with comprehensive research, including a website audit and competitive analysis to pinpoint critical usability issues and understand how similar organizations address design challenges. To align our redesign with the nonprofit's objectives, we held several stakeholder meetings to gain a deep understanding of the organizationâ€™s mission, goals, and target audience. These discussions provided valuable insights into their specific needs and expectations, guiding us in crafting solutions that would not only enhance functionality but also resonate with their core purpose and vision.

Objectives

After speaking with our stakeholders and understanding their goals with this redesign, we came up with a few objectives and design goals for this project.

Design Goals
  • Redesign all existing pages to elevate user interface (UI) and enhance overall user experience (UX), ensuring intuitive navigation and engaging visuals.
  • Introduce new features that include a dedicated page outlining roles and responsibilities for tutors and tutees, as well as an interactive global map showcasing their impact.
  • Streamline information architecture to improve content discoverability and user journey efficiency, maximizing the impact of educational offerings.
Website Audit

We conducted a comprehensive website audit to assess functionality, user experience, and overall effectiveness. Following our website audit, we identified several critical issues:

  • Excessive white space, impacting visual balance and usability.
  • Pages overloaded with text, potentially overwhelming visitors.
  • Inefficient navigation bar structure, complicating user journey.
  • Low-quality photos diminishing site aesthetics and professionalism.
  • Weak call-to-actions, reducing user engagement and conversion.
  • Text readability challenges, affecting content accessibility and user experience.

Addressing these findings will be crucial to enhancing overall functionality, user engagement, and the site's ability to effectively communicate with its audience.

Competitive Analysis

Our competitor analysis compared several key aspects of competing websites to assess how well they meet the needs of their target audiences in comparison to our nonprofit organization's website. The evaluation focused on user experience, functionality, content, and design.

Affinity Map

We conducted affinity mapping to systematically organize insights gathered from user interviews regarding our website's functionality and user experience.

Key Pain Points
  • Dissatisfaction with Visual Appeal and Mobile Performance
  • Lack of Clear Navigation
  • Overwhelmed with Excessive and Irrelevant Information

This process allows us to prioritize improvements effectively, ensuring our redesign addresses critical user needs and enhances overall usability.
