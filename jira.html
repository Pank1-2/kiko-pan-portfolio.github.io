Overview

In this 9-week project, my team and I conducted user interviews with industry professionals to identify pain points in the platforms they use daily for work.

For our redesign, we focused on:

  • Improving the onboarding experience to make Jira more intuitive for first-time users
  • Introducing an AI-powered chatbot to provide real-time assistance and guidance

Our redesign aims to streamline onboarding and enhance user support through AI, making Jira more accessible and user-friendly.

Objectives
Problem Statement

Jira is a widely used project management tool, but new users struggle with its complex onboarding process and lack of intuitive guidance. This results in frustration, wasted time, and decreased productivity.

Project Goals
  • Enhance Jira’s usability and accessibility for new users.
  • Reduce friction in the onboarding process for quicker user adoption.
  • Improve user engagement and efficiency for seamless team collaboration.
  • Address common pain points to create a user-friendly experience.
Background Research

Redesigning Jira is a challenging task due to its need to maintain consistency with the current branding and user experience. As a platform designed for a wide range of teams and industries, any redesign must remain flexible enough to cater to various use cases while improving usability. Our goal is to make Jira easier to use without compromising its adaptability for different teams.

To better understand the platform, we created a Jira account for our team and explored the interface. We found it difficult to navigate, particularly the onboarding process, which lacked clarity and actionable insights. To gain a deeper understanding, we watched YouTube tutorials, but even then, we realized that the platform could benefit from a more intuitive and user-friendly design.

User Personas

We developed these personas through user interviews and research, focusing on the needs, goals, and challenges of our key users, such as a marketing assistant and a product manager. These personas helped us better understand how users interact with the platform and ensured our redesign addressed their specific pain points.

Competitive Analysis

We analyzed tools like Trello, Asana, Monday.com, and Notion to inform our Jira redesign. For our project, we want to maintain the advanced project management features that Jira has, but use Notion’s clean user interface as inspiration to reduce confusion for users.

UX Flow

Our research revealed that Jira’s onboarding process lacks essential information. Given that Jira serves a wide range of teams, removing features isn’t the solution—instead, we need to enhance the onboarding experience.

How can we improve Jira’s onboarding to better accommodate different roles?

Our team developed two distinct UX flows for the onboarding process, each offering a unique approach to improving the experience. The two flows provide an onboarding process that helps junior team members become familiar with Jira and accustomed to completing complex functionalities.

UX Flow 1 – A role-specific walkthrough that tailors the onboarding experience based on the user’s job function. This guided introduction highlights essential features relevant to their role, ensuring a more intuitive and efficient onboarding process.

UX Flow 2 - This approach retains Jira’s existing onboarding while integrating an AI chatbot. The chatbot serves as an on-demand assistant, allowing users to ask questions and receive guidance on Jira’s features, improving accessibility and reducing the learning curve.

UI Sketches

During the sketching process, our team explored various layouts to enhance Jira’s onboarding experience, focusing on presenting key information, optimizing user flow, and improving understanding. Guided by our UX flow, we prioritized essential screens and analyzed competitors like Asana to identify effective onboarding elements. This helped us design a more customized experience tailored to different user roles.

Low Fidelity

Since Jira serves diverse users, a one-size-fits-all approach isn’t effective. We refined our UX flow, brainstormed ideas, and created two low-fidelity prototypes based on competitor analysis and sketches.

Prototype A maintains Jira’s login flow and builds on its role-selection feature with guided onboarding. Instead of overwhelming users, it highlights key tools through interactive walkthroughs and offers role-based templates to streamline setup. This approach enhances usability while preserving Jira’s flexibility.

Prototype B improves onboarding with an AI chatbot that offers real-time guidance, answering questions and providing step-by-step support. A “Click to Learn” feature delivers quick explanations, while the AI adapts to user behavior with proactive tips, ensuring a seamless learning experience.

Low Fidelity User Test

We conducted user testing with a Product Manager from Western Digital and a Software Engineer from Hologic to evaluate the usability of Prototype A and Prototype B.

Prototype A: Participants found it clear and easy to navigate, with one noting that the video tutorial idea was helpful. However, one participant found some buttons unclear, and both agreed some parts felt unnecessary.

Prototype B: One participant found it easier to navigate, while the other was confused by the page connections and unclear actions. This highlighted the need for better wireframing and clearer navigation.

Preferences: Both participants preferred a combination of features from both prototypes. Participant #1 favored Prototype B for its integration of interactive walkthroughs and an AI assistant, while Participant #2 preferred Prototype A's interactive tasks but suggested adding the AI assistant from Prototype B for additional guidance.

Overall, the user feedback emphasized the importance of combining interactive tasks with AI assistance to create a more effective onboarding experience.

High Fidelity Prototypes

For our high-fidelity prototype, we combined insights from user interviews and merged both versions of our UX flow. This prototype features both a customized onboarding process and an integrated AI chat for enhanced user support.

High Fidelity User Test

Due to time constraints, we conducted informal user interviews to test our hi-fi prototype. Here’s what we found:

Users appreciated elements from both prototypes. One participant liked the login page from Prototype 1 and the colors in Prototype 2, suggesting combining aspects like the highlighted words from Prototype 2 and the more straightforward roadmap screen from Prototype 1. Both participants recommended integrating the 'Chat with AI' feature into the final design.

The alternate version was seen as more seamless, especially with the template pop-up, while the original prototype’s guided walkthrough was appreciated for its structured onboarding. Participants suggested merging these strengths by combining the intuitive navigation of the alternate version with the guidance from the original. They also recommended making the AI chat feature less intrusive, proposing it as a smaller pop-up that users could expand.

Next Steps
  • Conduct additional user testing to gather more insights and refine the prototype.
  • Focus on brand consistency by aligning the design more closely with the current platform.
  • Enhance the visual elements to ensure the interface is intuitive and cohesive with the existing platform.
  • Improve the onboarding process to combine a guided flow with engaging, interactive elements.
